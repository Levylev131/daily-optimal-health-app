💪
Increases Strength & Power Output
Creatine replenishes ATP (your muscles' energy currency) faster during high-intensity efforts. This translates directly to more reps, heavier lifts, and greater peak power — especially during short, intense bursts like sprints and heavy sets.
📈
More Muscle Mass Over Time
By enabling more training volume and intensity, creatine indirectly drives greater hypertrophy. Studies consistently show 1–2 kg more lean mass gained over 4–12 weeks compared to placebo groups doing the same program.
⚡
Faster Recovery Between Sets
Higher phosphocreatine stores mean faster ATP resynthesis between sets. You'll feel fresher on later sets, which compounds into significantly greater total volume over the course of a session.
🧠
Cognitive Benefits
The brain uses creatine too. Research shows improvements in working memory, processing speed, and mental fatigue resistance — especially noticeable under sleep deprivation or high cognitive stress.
🏃
Improved High-Intensity Performance
Effective for any sport involving repeated short bursts: sprinting, football, basketball, martial arts, rowing, swimming, climbing. Less impactful for pure endurance (marathons, long-distance cycling).
🦴
Bone Health & Aging
Emerging research suggests creatine may support bone density and help preserve muscle mass during aging, making it valuable for older adults — not just athletes.
Bottom line: Creatine has some of the most consistent, replicated evidence in all of sports science. The effect sizes are real and clinically meaningful — not marginal.

Smoothie
Creatine is nearly tasteless and slightly chalky. It doesn't fully dissolve in cold water — shake well. Avoid boiling hot liquids as heat can degrade it.
🍽️ Meals That Maximize Creatine Uptake
Insulin is the key — it helps shuttle creatine into muscle cells. Pairing creatine with carbs + protein can increase muscle uptake by up to 25–40% compared to taking it alone. You don't need a massive meal — a moderate carb/protein combo is enough.
⏱️ Timing tip: Eat the meal first, then take your creatine within 30–60 minutes while insulin is elevated — or simply mix it directly into a carb + protein drink.
  • ✅
    Most studied supplement in sports science
    Over 500 peer-reviewed studies. No long-term harm found in healthy individuals at standard doses.
  • ✅
    Safe for kidneys in healthy people
    Creatine raises creatinine levels in bloodwork (a normal byproduct) — sometimes misread as kidney stress. It does NOT damage kidneys in people without pre-existing kidney disease.
  • ✅
    No need to cycle
    No evidence that cycling creatine (taking breaks) provides any benefit. Continuous use is safe and effective.
  • ⚠️
    Consult your doctor if you have kidney disease
    If you have pre-existing kidney problems or a family history of renal disease, get medical clearance before starting.
  • ⚠️
    Loading phase may cause GI discomfort
    20g/day during loading can cause bloating or loose stools in some. Split into 4–5 smaller doses across the day to reduce this risk.
  • ⚠️
    Water weight gain is normal and expected
    Creatine draws water into muscle cells. Expect 0.5–2 kg of scale increase in the first 1–2 weeks. This is not fat — it's intramuscular fluid that makes muscles look fuller.
  • ❌
    Not recommended during pregnancy
    Insufficient safety data during pregnancy and breastfeeding. Avoid supplementation.
  • 💧
    Stay well hydrated
    Because creatine pulls water into muscles, aim for at least 2–3L of water per day while supplementing.
⚠️ Quality matters: Buy from brands with third-party testing (NSF, Informed Sport, Labdoor). Pure creatine monohydrate powder is the cheapest and most effective form — avoid expensive "creatine blends."
🏆
Creatine Monohydrate — The Gold Standard
The original and still the best. Most studied, cheapest, and equally effective as any other form. All the landmark research is based on this. Stick with it.
💊
Micronized Creatine Monohydrate
Same compound, ground into finer particles. Dissolves better in water and may cause slightly less GI discomfort. Good choice if you have stomach sensitivity. Equally effective.
🧪
Creatine HCL
Highly soluble and gentle on the stomach. Marketed as needing a lower dose (1–2g), but evidence doesn't show it outperforms monohydrate. Significantly more expensive per serving.
⚗️
Creatine Ethyl Ester (CEE)
Popular in the early 2000s. Claims of superior absorption were never proven — studies found it actually converts to creatinine (waste) faster than monohydrate. Not recommended.
🔬
Buffered Creatine (Kre-Alkalyn)
Marketed as more stomach-stable. Head-to-head studies show no performance difference vs. monohydrate. Significantly more expensive with no proven upside.
Verdict: Creatine monohydrate (or micronized if you have GI sensitivity). All other forms are marketing, not better science.
